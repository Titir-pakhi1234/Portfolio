1. “Revisiting Disc-Jet Coupling in Black Hole X-ray Binaries: On the Nature of Disc Dynamics and Jet Velocity”: We attempt to study the disc-jet connection in sixteen outbursts from thirteen black hole X-ray binary sources (BH-XRBs). We utilise RXTE, HXMT and AstroSat data of such BH-XRBs. We study the correlation between the spectro-temporal parameters such as QPO rms, time lag and normalised Comptonised flux to interpret the evolution of the corona during the state transition, coinciding with the appearance of radio flares. Addtionally, we constrain the jet velocity and observe a correlation between jet velocity and X-ray flux. Finally we observe a correlation between X-ray luminosity and radio luminosity.

2. Unveiling Disc-Jet Coupling in BH-XRBs: Multi-band and Multi-mission perspective.

3. In-depth analysis of ω, κ and ρ variability classes in GRS 1915+105 using multiple missions.

4. Complex modelling of LFQPOs in Swift J1727.8-1613 and its spectral properties.

Research & Publications

I am actively engaged in a comprehensive research project centered on Black Hole X-ray Binaries, with a particular focus on analyzing multiple mission X-ray data such as RXTE, AstroSat, HXMT and XPoSat from multiple black hole binary sources. My work encompasses an in-depth examination of both spectral and timing properties, with a keen interest in unraveling the intricate dynamics of the accretion disk and understanding the connection to jet formation. I am also conducting a parallel investigation involving ω, κ and ρ variability classes observations of GRS 1915+105 using data from RXTE, AstroSat and HXMT, further strengthening my expertise in high-energy astrophysics.
